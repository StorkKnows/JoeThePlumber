  1. How can I prevent my pipes from freezing?
  2. If you pipes burst, what should I do to stop the flooding
  3. My washing machine smells like sewer. What can I do?
  4. What size air conditioner do I need?
  5. My hot water keeps running out. Do I need a larger water heater?

A1: Begin by insulating the pipes that are located on the outside walls. And, if you have had previous problems with freezing, leave a faucet trickling with warm water.


A2: In the event of a pipe burst, turn ooff the closest shut-off valve or your main water shut-off valve untill a professional can arrive.


A3:Front loading washers sometimes develop mold around the thick gasket that seals the front door. Inspect the gasket itself and use bleach to disinfect.


A4:A load calculation must be preformed on your home to determine the proper system size that meets all the pysical requirements. Ultimately, the size determination should be made by a qualified air conditioning specialist.


A5:There are many reasons why you may be running out of hot water. Before installing a larger heater, one of our technicians can analyze yuor options
